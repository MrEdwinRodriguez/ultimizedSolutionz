When one comes across the word ERP, he may wonder that what ERP stands for? ERP stands for Enterprise Resource Planning and is considered the best and most widely used software resource for business companies to go for - considering its wide range of resourceful applications that are available all in one. ERP is the perfect software for apt and effective business management these days. It is very handy and useful software and solutions by which a company can easily store its data and manage it on every stage of the business. ERP software and solutions can be used in every department of the company, such as marketing and sales departments, manufacturing departments, finance departments, shipping, product and planning departments, human resources and the inventory management departments. The most important feature of ERP software is its central database which is a shared one among all the departments of the company and it is very supportive because it has multiple functions that are used in different units of the company. ERP software also offers the option of synchronized reporting and of automation which can be very useful for the employees in a company using ERP to pull out the required data and reports from the database as and when needed.

Enterprise resource planning is considered a must go software and solutions for all the major business around the world in the present era which is flooded with a lot of booming technological advancements. At the early stage of ERP, the enterprise software companies used to target and give their services to the large enterprises around the world especially in the west but these days the small enterprises are also using the ERP software increasingly making better, easier to use and more customized than before.

ERP software and solutions are to a very useful and essential tool for an organization because it facilitates all the departments of an organization at the same time and provides them with error free services. The ERP systems have different development from that of the traditional systems because it is comprised of a variety of hardware configurations and computer hardware which uses a database for information storage and delivery. The enterprise resource planning software and solutions have some stand out features that make it unique and different from all other software systems. Hadoop, Big Data and Business Intelligence

What is Hadoop?

Hadoop is a programming system that handles mass information over numerous servers. It has become one of most suitable alternatives for organizations because it can efficiently deal with enormous amounts of information in less time. Electronic organizations (i.e. Facebook, Amazon, eBay, etc.) have utilized top-of-the-line Hadoop applications to handle their extensive information sets. Ultimately, Hadoop is a system that deals with mass amounts of information and makes data more accessible to those who use it on a regular basis.

Tools Required to Accomplish Business Intelligence

Business Intelligence (BI) depends upon Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), in order to screen and foresee execution of various associations. The KPIs are then related to the operational execution inside a Business Intelligence Application. It allows a business to see the ranges of influence. When information is being organized without KPIs and BI, the problems involve conflicts of definitions, incorrect data flow, duplication and productivity issues.

Data Warehousing is another tool to help with Business Intelligence, as the data stores need to be adjusted for the various zones and departments of a business. When the data is too heavily fragmented, it can lead to problems with access, as well with synchronization. Hadoop has gone beyond the expectation of success. What Hadoop and Business Intelligence can do as a team is impressive!

The impact is significant!

Real Time Information: There is an end-to-end data lineage that allows organizations to see information from start to finish. It streamlines data and offers continuous support.

Business Process Execution: Operational methodology can be executed more effectively, allowing procedures to be streamlined and productivity to increase substantially.

More Business Intelligence: When there is more BI within data, there is more efficiency within operations. It allows everyone to access data from anywhere, in a secure fashion.

Front Office Systems: There are more analytics and data mining taking place within front office systems, allowing data to be accessed faster, providing a higher level of customer service – as well as the potential to interact more effectively with customers, increasing sales.

The impact will vary based upon the actual tools being used.

Advantages to Customers

One of the most important is that of information quality. It is crucial for businesses to have a higher-quality level of information and this is possible through organizing the data and using various tools.

The Data Warehouse architecture is more sophisticated than ever and this allows businesses to not only get the data they need, but have the data analyzed. This means the data is able to work within the system to produce reports and compare against other data and trends to provide more information to business managers.

What do you do next?

Figure out the best way to organize data and find the data you need quickly. Changing relationships can be discovered through a logical structure of data, which is where Data Warehousing comes in. Business Intelligence is also important to ensure data is analyzed effectively, allowing managers to make more informed decisions.

A Hadoop database is just what you need.
